Lvl. 1: Road to Jupiter


start mission

Dou you have what it takes to get from Earth to Jupiter? In your first assignment a powerful launch helps to make things easy, but can you use this occasion to shorten your time of flight?. What planet will help you getting there? The Earth? Venus? Mars?

Mission details

  • Starting epoch: from 2017-Sep-04 to 2023-Feb-25, corrsponding to [58000, 60000] as Modified Julian Date (MJD)
  • Launcher: DV capability: 6127 m/sed
  • Spacecraft:
    • Initial (wet) mass: 2200 kg
    • Propellant: 1000 kg
    • Specific impulse: 308 s
    • Maximum thrust: n/a
  • End conditions:
    • Target: Jupiter
    • Spacecraft mass ≥ 1200 kg
    • Mission duration ≤ 15 years
  • Score system: 100 point are assigned for each percentual point of propellant and time of flight saved

Lvl. 2: Grand tour


start mission

In your second mission, you start captured in the Jupiter system with very little propellant and are requested to deliver precise maps of the Galilean moons surface within 243 days. The more the scientific interest of the mapped areas the more points you get. Your initial position is at Ganymede. Good luck!! (as if it was about luck)

Mission details

  • Starting epoch: 2020-Jan-01, corrsponding to 58849 MJD as Modified Julian Date (MJD)
  • Starting velocity: [3000, -2500, 500] m/s relative to Ganymede
  • Spacecraft:
    • Initial mass: 2000 kg
    • Propellant: 20 kg
    • Specific impulse: 2300 s
    • Maximum thrust: 0.15 N
  • End conditions:
    • Spacecraft mass ≥ 1980 kg
    • Mission duration ≤ 243 days
  • Score system: Points are awarded for each face of a galilean moon visited. A moon face is considered as visited if the periapsis of the relative fly-by hyperbola is aligned with it. You can see the exact point visited on the surface as a small dot appearing on it at each fly-by.

Lvl. 3: Space Roomba


start mission

In your third mission you are requested to help cleaning up the space around Earth. A number of big pieces of junk are threatening the use of the orbital space and a "space roomba" mission has been designed to be launched from the International Space Station to intercept the largest possible number of junk pieces in the given timeframe and propellant budget.

Mission details

  • Starting epoch: from 2009-Jun-18 to 2009-Jun-19 corresponding to [58849, 58850] MJD as Modified Julian Date (MJD)
  • Spacecraft:
    • Initial mass: 2000 kg
    • Propulsion: 1000 kg
    • Specific impulse: 250 s
    • Maximum thrust: n/a
  • Launcher: The spacecarft is released from the ISS (International Space Station) with a release mechanism that allows for a DV of 2163 m/s
  • End conditions:
    • All debris are cleaned
    • Spacecraft mass ≥ 1000 kg
    • Mission duration ≤ 30 days
  • Score system: Each piece of debris is scored one point. The percentage of fuel remaining is also added to the score.
